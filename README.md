## Selected projects and field experience

### TPChange project — airborne aerosol measurements and sensor development

I am currently working in the TPChange project at the Technical University of Darmstadt. My work focuses on developing, testing, and deploying aerosol sampling systems for high-altitude atmospheric measurements.

A major part of this work is the development of the **TuDa Integrated Aerosol Sampling System**, which combines different sampling approaches with sensor control, data acquisition, and field operation workflows. The system includes instrument design, Raspberry Pi-based control, Python-based data handling, calibration, troubleshooting, documentation, and preparation for airborne deployment.

This project has given me hands-on experience in building measurement systems that need to work reliably under demanding field conditions.

Repository: [TuDa_IASS_Aerosol_Sampler](https://github.com/IsmayilSadath/TuDa_IASS_Aerosol_Sampler)

---

### TPEx campaign — airborne field measurements over Europe

As part of the TPEx campaign, I supported airborne research measurements over Central and Northern Europe. The campaign focused on aerosol and trace-gas observations in the upper troposphere and lower stratosphere transition region.

My work included preparing and operating aerosol sampling instrumentation, supporting pre-flight checks, handling samples, checking data quality during the campaign, and helping connect the measurements with meteorological conditions and transport pathways.

This experience strengthened my interest in field-based environmental monitoring, sensor platforms, and the use of observational data to understand atmospheric processes.

---

### IITM / SAFAR project — urban air-quality monitoring in Delhi

Before my current work, I worked with the SAFAR air-quality programme at the Indian Institute of Tropical Meteorology. The project involved continuous monitoring of particulate matter, trace gases, and meteorological conditions across Delhi.

I supported observational work, data screening, quality control, anomaly checks, and interpretation of pollution episodes. This gave me early experience with large environmental monitoring datasets and the practical challenges of working with real-world sensor networks.

The SAFAR project helped build my foundation in environmental data analysis, field measurements, and scientific reporting.

---

### Aerosol Chase — mobile environmental measurements

During my research work in Sweden, I contributed to the Aerosol Chase project, a mobile measurement experiment designed to follow air masses and study the evolution of aerosol properties during atmospheric transport.

I worked with mobile instrumentation, power-supply solutions, field operation, and data interpretation linking particle observations with meteorological conditions and source regions.

---

### Drone-supported dust measurements

I have also participated in a drone-based dust measurement campaign focused on transported mineral dust, including Saharan dust intrusions. My work involved field preparation, instrument operation, troubleshooting, sample collection, and coordination of daily measurement activities.

This experience is one reason I am interested in drone-supported environmental observations and remote-sensing applications.
